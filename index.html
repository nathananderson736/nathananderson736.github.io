  • About My Portfolio

    Design was one of the key aspects in mind for my portfolio. A web portfolio provided me with an awesome opportunity to create my own visual identity from the sources that inspire me, but with such vast access to inspiration, came responsibility/balance. I wanted to achieve a style I was going to love, but I also wanted to show my care for the smaller details like, balance, consistency, readability, things that add up in a big way towards creating trust.

    Font Choice

    I tend to get an energetic vibe from listening to rock a lot with its punchiness. Creating a visual identity was one of my goals for my portfolio, so I knew I wanted that energetic vibe to be visually part of my design. Taking away the excess that experimenting gave me, it boiled down to using a font with a slight imperfect appearance. I found it was the most simple and effective way to express energy and character without forgetting my target audience.

  • Advanced Client-Side Project - Sticky Notes Application

    Visit Website

    Goal

    For our advanced client-side project, we needed to research modern web technologies such as, HTML5/CSS3 as well as geolocation api or local storage, to create a mobile first optimized website that would showcase modern web technologies. I saw local storage as a chance to fulfil my desire to create something interactive and useful, similar to an app or a site that relies on a database. Memo/note apps already exist, Google Keep being my favourite, but it wasn't going to stop me from making my own one. I liked the idea of creating my own UI, even more when it has purpose and integrates with our lives.

    Design

    It wasn't my desire to straight copy sites like Google Keep or Evernote for my notes website. For inspiration I was drawn more towards Google Keeps interface. For anyone who wanted to use my site, including myself, I wanted the interface to be minimal, avoiding scattered UI elements that would detract from the experience of feeling like you're using your own personal board.

    Tools

    • HTML5
    • CSS3
    • Javascript/jQuery
    • Local Storage

  • Client Project - Kate Larkindale Personal Site

    Visit Website

    Goal

    Our end of year client project required us to search for an actual client to create a website for. We could take on the client's requirements, so long as they met our assessment requirements. After phoning around seeking any small cinema as my client, I got not a cinema, but the manager of one who needed a personal site made. Kate Larkindale, manager at the time of Penthouse Cinema in Wellington, is currently and has been for some time, a writer and film reviewer. Kate needed a personal site as an improvement over her blog, which she uses for blog posts and to display her books.

    My Assessment Requirements

    • Responsive/Mobile First Design.
    • Use PHP and MySQL to interact with a database.
    • Use two of the following three:
      • Search Engine Optimization
      • Enhanced for Accessibility
      • Use an API

    Kate's Requirements

    • Site design that reflect Kate's style
    • Display catalogue of Kate's books with links to online retailers
    • Latest book needed to be highly visible
    • Personal account to add, update and delete books displayed
    • Bio for those unware of who Kate Larkindale is
